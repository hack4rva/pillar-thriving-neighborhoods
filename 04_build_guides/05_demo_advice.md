# Demo Advice — Thriving Neighborhoods

## 90-second structure

- Problem (10s): A Richmond resident who lives two blocks from a new apartment development had no idea it was being proposed. The planning meeting was three months ago. There's no easy way to find out what's being built near you.
- User (10s): Meet a Richmond homeowner — she has a smartphone and checks her neighborhood Facebook group, but has never used Legistar.
- What we built (20s): Show the working flow — enter an address, see nearby development proposals on a map, click through to a plain-language summary with a comment deadline.
- Sources (20s): Point to the Legistar source link on each result. Show the "View official record" button. Explain that data comes from Richmond's public City Council records.
- Limits (10s): This tool helps residents discover proposals; it does not predict outcomes, make official recommendations, or replace the formal comment process.
- Next (20s): We need a departmental champion in Planning to confirm data accuracy and explore a notification feature. The data pipeline is open source.

## Tips

- Use real Richmond addresses, real Legistar cases, and real neighborhood names. Nothing kills demo credibility faster than "123 Main Street" or placeholder text.
- Hardcode 5–8 curated examples if the live API is unreliable — reliability matters more than live data for the demo.
- Show a source badge or "from Legistar" label on every result card.
- Show the comment deadline prominently — this is the clearest demonstration of user value.
- Keep claims narrow: "helps residents discover" not "keeps residents informed of all development."
- Prepare a fallback: if the map or API fails, have screenshots ready and narrate over them.
- Put disclaimers and source labels on screen, not just in speech — judges notice.
- End with a concrete ask: "We need the City to confirm Legistar API access and a staff owner in Planning."

## What not to say

- "This will prevent displacement" — too broad; cannot demonstrate causality in 48 hours.
- "This gives residents real-time updates" — only say this if you have confirmed real-time data pipeline.
- "This is what the City should build" — do not speak for the City.
- "Our AI summarizes planning documents accurately" — say "our AI-assisted summaries are reviewed for accuracy and link back to the official document."
