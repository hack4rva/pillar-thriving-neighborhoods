# C4 Services Gaps – 2026 Assessment

*Prepared 2026-03-24* 

## Executive Summary
- **CJADC2 lacks a unified investment-tracking framework**, leaving many proposed data-sharing initiatives without measurable milestones. The GAO found that the DoD has yet to establish a framework to effectively guide investments and track progress [1]. *Action*: Adopt the GAO-recommended CJADC2 Framework within FY 2027. 
- **FMN spirals remain fragmented**; NATO's 2025 HCSS assessment shows interoperability gaps delay 25% of data exchanges, especially in Baltic Air Policing and land-domain links [2]. *Action*: Establish a joint FMN-Governance Board to enforce common standards by 2027 Q3. 
- **Zero-Trust rollout is uneven**; The DoD Zero Trust Strategy identifies foundational pillars and tenets, but implementation across legacy systems remains incomplete [3]. *Action*: Prioritize "Never Trust, Always Verify" and "Unified Analytics" for legacy C4 platforms by FY 2028. 
- **EMS spectrum congestion is accelerating**; the 2020 DoD EMS Superiority Strategy flags a "congested, contested, and constrained" electromagnetic operational environment (EMOE) [4]. *Action*: Fund dynamic spectrum-sharing pilots with commercial partners starting FY 2026. 
- **JWCC procurement lag**; DISA's JWCC-Next enterprise cloud program solicitation plans aim for the first quarter of calendar year 2026, indicating ongoing transitions for cloud-native services [5]. *Action*: Create a joint JWCC KPI dashboard to monitor migration progress quarterly. 
- **Project Overmatch network deployment** reached 3 carrier strike groups as of March 2024, yet full fleet integration faces budgeting and scheduling hurdles [6]. *Action*: Secure FY 2026 supplemental funding and accelerate integration testing on the remaining carriers. 

These gaps collectively erode command-and-control tempo, increase cyber-risk exposure, and jeopardize U.S./NATO joint warfighting effectiveness. 

## Scope & Methodology
- **Geographic focus:** United States Department of Defense (DoD) and NATO alliance. 
- **Time horizon:** Data collected January 2023 – March 2026; forward-looking projections to 2030. 
- **Sources:** GAO reports, DoD Zero-Trust Strategy, DoD EMS Superiority Strategy, NATO FMN spiral documents, DISA/JWCC briefs, Project Overmatch press releases, and peer-reviewed analyses (CSIS, HCSS). 
- **Analytical framework:** Gap analysis matrix (Capability × Maturity × Investment), cross-referencing interoperability, security, and spectrum constraints.

## Key Capability Gaps

### 4.1 Joint All-Domain Command & Control (CJADC2)
- **Finding:** The GAO identified that the DoD has yet to establish a framework to effectively guide investments and track progress for CJADC2 [1]. Furthermore, the DoD needs an approach to consolidate and share lessons learned from ongoing experimentation [1].
- **Implication:** Service-level pilots progress unevenly, and duplicated efforts waste resources while failing to achieve unified command and control goals. 
- **Action:** Establish a CJADC2 Investment & Metrics Board reporting to the Secretary of Defense; mandate quarterly portfolio reviews and a unified data-sharing KPI dashboard. 

### 4.2 Federated Mission Networking (FMN) & NATO-wide Interoperability
- **Finding:** The HCSS 2025 NATO C4ISR paper notes interoperability gaps that delay 25% of data exchanges, as seen in 2024 Baltic Air Policing [2]. European underinvestment and cultural resistance to AI-driven command and control exacerbate these challenges [2].
- **Implication:** Coalition missions experience latency that can degrade decision cycles in contested environments, risking operational bottlenecks [2]. 
- **Action:** Create a NATO FMN Alignment Cell (joint US-NATO) to certify baseline standards for all domains and to accelerate Link 22 integration targeted for 2030 [2]. 

### 4.3 Zero-Trust Architecture (ZTA) Implementation
- **Finding:** The DoD Zero Trust Strategy outlines foundational pillars and tenets, but achieving "Advanced Zero Trust" across all legacy systems remains a challenge [3]. NIST notes that the current state of the ZTA ecosystem is not mature enough for widespread adoption without interoperability issues [7].
- **Implication:** Legacy C4 nodes remain vulnerable to lateral movement and compromised networks. 
- **Action:** Prioritize "Never Trust, Always Verify" for all C4 services by FY 2028; fund a Zero-Trust Integration Lab to pilot retrofits on legacy command posts. 

### 4.4 Electromagnetic Spectrum (EMS) Superiority & PNT Resilience
- **Finding:** The 2020 DoD EMS Superiority Strategy warns of a "congested, contested, and constrained" electromagnetic operational environment [4]. Additionally, 70% of NATO's undersea cables are exposed, highlighting severe infrastructure vulnerabilities [2].
- **Implication:** Adversary electronic warfare could deny or degrade PNT and communications for joint operations, eroding the U.S. military's ability to sense, command, and control [4]. 
- **Action:** Expand dynamic spectrum-sharing testbeds with commercial 5G providers; allocate funding for hardened PNT infrastructure by FY 2027. 

### 4.5 Joint Warfighting Cloud Capability (JWCC) & Data-Centric Security
- **Finding:** DISA is prepping for the JWCC-Next enterprise cloud program, with plans to issue a solicitation in the first quarter of calendar year 2026 [5]. The modification aims to help military services more easily move applications to the cloud [8].
- **Implication:** Cloud-based C4 analytics remain siloed during the transition, limiting AI-enabled decision support across the joint force. 
- **Action:** Deploy a JWCC Data-Centric Security Scorecard (aligned with NIST 800-207) and tie JWCC contract incentives to achieving a "Secure-by-Design" rating. 

### 4.6 Project Overmatch Network Deployment
- **Finding:** Overmatch networking was installed on 3 carrier strike groups as of March 2024, with the Navy seeking $192 million for the project in fiscal 2024 [6]. 
- **Implication:** Incomplete carrier-wide connectivity hampers real-time sensor fusion and slows kill-chain times for carriers lacking Overmatch capabilities. 
- **Action:** Secure FY 2026 supplemental appropriations and mandate integration test milestones at the fleet level by FY 2027.
