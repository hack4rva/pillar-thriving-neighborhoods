## Key Findings

### Dealership-to-Consumer Demo Sales
BMW officially permitted U.S. dealerships to sell their demo i4 vehicles starting in 2022-08 [1]. A communication from a local BMW dealership confirmed this policy change, indicating that BMW is now allowing dealerships to sell their demo vehicles directly to consumers [1]. This opened a new supply channel for lightly-used i4s, often priced below standard retail.

### Reliability & Predicted Longevity
The i4 consistently scores among the most reliable EVs in recent consumer reporting. Consumer Reports data indicates that the i4 is ranked as 5/5 on predicted reliability [2]. Furthermore, Consumer Reports rates the vehicle at 82/100, positioning it as the most reliable EV and close to the most reliable vehicle overall in their listings [3]. This high reliability reduces total-ownership cost and supports the credibility of demo-vehicle durability claims.

### Consumer Sentiment & Real-World Experience
Early-owner reviews of the 2025 i4 are overwhelmingly positive regarding comfort and range. Consumer reviews on Edmunds highlight that the comfort level is outstanding and the battery distance is an unbelievable 300+ miles [4]. Owners also report receiving multiple compliments on their choice of color and note that the vehicle rides very nicely [4].

### Pricing & Financial Terms of Demo Units
Demo i4s are being offered with aggressive lease terms that can undercut standard retail financing. For example, EliteBMW advertises a $699.00/month lease for a DEMO 2025 BMW i4 eDrive40, featuring a 0.00% APR over a 48-month term with $0 due at delivery [5]. The promotion noted 10 units in stock and a $12,000 rebate [5]. While the low monthly payment may attract price-sensitive buyers, the short-term nature of leases requires careful consideration of long-term costs.

### Risk Factors Specific to Demo Vehicles
While demo vehicles offer financial incentives, they come with specific risks. Vehicles found on the lot are often dealer demos or customer cancellations, and demo vehicles can sometimes be "pretty stripped" of optional features [6]. 

| Risk | Description | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|------------|
| Higher mileage | Demo cars are driven for test drives and showroom runs; mileage can exceed typical "new" thresholds. | Request a full service record and verify mileage before purchase. |
| Stripped options | Dealers may remove accessories or order base models for demo purposes to reduce cost [6]. | Inspect the vehicle checklist and negotiate reinstatement or discount for missing options. |
| Warranty transfer uncertainty | Some manufacturers limit warranty coverage on demo units. | Confirm warranty terms in writing; consider purchasing an extended warranty. |

*Takeaway: Buyers must actively verify the specific configuration and history of a demo vehicle to ensure the discounted price justifies any missing features or accumulated wear.*

## Conclusions & Recommendations

1. **Overall Credibility:** The majority of claims about i4 demo vehicles are highly credible. Official policy changes allowing demo sales are well-documented [1], and the vehicle's exceptional reliability scores are corroborated by Consumer Reports data [2] [3]. 

2. **Action for Buyers:** 
 - **Verify mileage** and request a detailed service log before signing any lease or purchase agreement.
 - **Confirm warranty coverage** to ensure the demo status does not negatively impact the standard manufacturer warranty.
 - **Inspect the feature list carefully**, as demo vehicles may be stripped of premium options [6]. Negotiate a price reduction reflecting the actual configuration.

3. **Action for Dealerships:** 
 - Publish transparent mileage and option-list disclosures on demo-vehicle listings to build trust and accelerate sales cycles.
 - Continue leveraging aggressive financing terms (like 0.00% APR) to move demo inventory efficiently [5].
