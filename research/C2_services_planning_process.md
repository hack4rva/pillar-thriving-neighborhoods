# C2 Services Planning Process

*(Revised 2026-03-24 – placeholders removed, dates validated, sources added)* 

--- 

## Introduction
The Command-and-Control (C2) Services Planning Process defines how joint and multinational forces develop, document, and sustain the communications and information-system services that enable effective C2. It aligns the **Joint Publication (JP) 5-0 – Joint Planning** and **JP 6-0 – Joint Communications System** frameworks with NATO architecture guidance to ensure interoperable, resilient service delivery throughout the operation lifecycle. 

> **Key point:** A unified process reduces planning gaps that historically caused delayed communications capability deployment in multinational exercises. 

--- 

## Scope & Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **C2 Services** | Integrated communications, network, and information-system capabilities that support the commander's decision cycle (planning, execution, assessment) [1]. |
| **J-6** | Joint staff functional area responsible for communications-system planning, acquisition, and sustainment [1]. |
| **Annex K** | The official annex to the Joint Operation Plan (JOP) that details the Command, Control, Communications, and Computer (C4) systems required for the mission [1]. |
| **Service-Oriented Architecture (SOA)** | Design approach endorsed by NATO (NAFv4) for modular, reusable C2 services across domains [2]. |

## Planning Phase Overview

The process mirrors the **Joint Planning Process (JPP)** (seven steps) and inserts a dedicated communications-planning loop (steps 1-5) as described in JP 6-0 [3] [1]. 

1. **Planning Initiation** – Define scope, objectives, and assign the J-6 lead [3]. 
2. **Mission Analysis** – Capture commander's intent, concept of operations (CONOPS), and **Commander's Critical Information Requirements (CCIRs)** [1]. 
3. **Course-of-Action (COA) Development** – Generate alternative C2-service packages for each operational COA [3]. 
4. **COA Analysis & Wargaming** – Assess feasibility, interoperability, and security of each service package [3]. 
5. **COA Comparison & Approval** – Select the preferred C2-service solution; document decisions in **Annex K** [1]. 
6. **Plan or Order Development** – Produce the Joint Operation Plan (JOP) and associated **C2 Service Estimate** [3]. 
7. **Assessment & Feedback** – Continuous monitoring of service performance; feed lessons back into future JPP cycles [3]. 

> **Why it matters:** Embedding communications planning early (Step 2) prevents "last-minute" capability shortfalls that have plagued legacy operations.

## Communications Planning Integration (JP 6-0)

JP 6-0 outlines **five core planning areas** that must be addressed for every C2 service [1]: 

| Planning Area | Core Questions | Typical Output |
|---------------|----------------|----------------|
| **Mission Analysis** | What are the CCIRs? What is the C2 task organization? | *Communications System Estimate* (baseline requirements). |
| **Information-Requirements Analysis** | Which data links, bandwidth, and latency levels are needed? | *Information-Requirement Matrix*. |
| **Interoperability, Compatibility & Supportability** | Are service interfaces NATO-compliant? Is legacy equipment supported? | *Compatibility Gap Report*. |
| **Capability Analysis** | Which platforms (SATCOM, EMS, DODIN) will deliver the required services? | *Capability Allocation Table*. |
| **Asset Allocation** | How are communications assets phased and sequenced? | *Phased Deployment Schedule*. |

Key guidance from JP 6-0 stresses **continuous feasibility assessment** and **modular packaging** to enable rapid re-configuration [1].

## Documentation & Annex K

- **Annex K Content** – Lists every communications system function, associated frequencies, security controls (COMSEC, cybersecurity), and responsible units (J-6, component J-6s) [1]. 
- **Formatting Requirement** – Follow the template in JP 5-0 and NATO AJP-5 guidance for multinational ops [3] [4]. 
- **Version Control** – Each annex version must carry a *Revision Date* in **YYYY-MM** format (e.g., **2025-11**). 

> **Note:** If a required datum cannot be sourced, the annex should state **"Data not available – pending stakeholder input"** rather than leaving a blank field.

## Governance & Compliance

- **Regulatory Reference** – Washington State Register **06-20-035** (effective **2006-12**) mandates that any relevant plan include a **compliance schedule** linking to WAC 173-182-310 through 440 requirements [5]. 
- **NATO Architecture Alignment** – The plan must reference the **NATO Architecture Framework (NAFv4)** to verify that services map to defined **C2 Architecture Tags** [2]. 
- **Joint Doctrine Review** – Prior to execution, the Joint Operations Center (JOC) conducts a **C2 Services Review Board** to certify that all documentation meets JP 5-0 and JP 6-0 standards.
