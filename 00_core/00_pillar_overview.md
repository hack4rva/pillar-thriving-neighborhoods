# Pillar Overview — Thriving Neighborhoods

## Purpose
This repository is a markdown knowledge base for teams working on the Thriving Neighborhoods pillar in the Richmond Civic Hackathon. It helps teams understand the problem space, review problem statements, explore public data, and shape realistic weekend MVPs.

## Pillar framing
Thriving Neighborhoods focuses on making neighborhood development, housing investment, and planning processes more transparent and accessible to Richmond residents, including:
- making it easier for residents to discover and understand development proposals in their neighborhoods
- helping City staff monitor compliance with affordable housing commitments
- translating technical planning documents and council records into accessible language
- enabling earlier and more meaningful resident participation in the planning process
- visualizing neighborhood change over time using public data

## Why this matters
When residents cannot easily discover development proposals, they miss critical windows for public comment. When affordable housing compliance is hard to monitor, public investments may not deliver on their promises. Transparency and accessibility are prerequisites for equitable civic participation.

## Useful prototype directions
Prototypes that often help in this pillar:
- make Legistar records easier to find and understand
- make development proposals discoverable by address or neighborhood
- help staff monitor and track housing investment compliance
- reveal patterns in neighborhood change using public GIS and parcel data
- translate planning and zoning language into plain English

## Less useful directions
Prototypes that tend to struggle in a weekend:
- require policy changes or new legal authority
- require integration with official City planning or licensing systems
- make legal, zoning, or housing compliance determinations
- depend on confidential contract data or internal City databases
- claim to predict whether a development project will be approved
