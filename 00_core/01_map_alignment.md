# MAP Alignment — Thriving Neighborhoods

## What is MAP?
MAP (Master Action Plan) is Richmond's strategic plan for equitable city growth. The Thriving Neighborhoods pillar connects directly to several MAP goals around housing, community engagement, and transparent governance.

## Key MAP connections

### Equitable neighborhoods
Richmond's MAP commits to ensuring that the benefits of neighborhood investment reach all residents — including long-term residents at risk of displacement. Transparency tools that surface development proposals early give communities the ability to engage before decisions are finalized.

### Housing affordability
MAP includes goals around expanding affordable housing options. Technology that helps the City monitor compliance with affordability commitments supports accountability for public investments in housing programs.

### Resident voice in planning
MAP recognizes the need for more inclusive civic participation. Development discovery tools that notify residents before comment periods close — in plain language — directly advance MAP's civic engagement goals.

### Transparent government
MAP calls for government processes that are open, accessible, and understandable to all residents. Making Legistar records and planning documents readable to non-experts aligns with this goal.

## What hackathon projects cannot do
Projects cannot:
- change MAP priorities or City policy
- commit the City to new housing obligations
- represent official City positions on development proposals
- replace formal public comment processes

These remain within the authority of elected officials and City departments.
