Top 10 Research Questions

1) Which of the two targeted Pillar 2 statements reflects the clearest, most research-ready Richmond problem for a weekend software prototype?

2) Where exactly do Richmond residents get stuck when trying to find and understand development proposals in their neighborhoods — what do they encounter on Legistar, the City website, and Planning Commission materials?

3) What is the actual structure and accessibility of Richmond's Legistar data — is the Granicus Web API available, what fields are populated, and how reliable is address data for geocoding?

4) What GIS layers on the Richmond GeoHub most directly support a development proposal lookup tool — which layers are current, which are static snapshots, and how does access work?

5) What does the City's affordable housing compliance monitoring workflow look like today — what systems, spreadsheets, and manual processes are involved, and where are the biggest inefficiencies?

6) What public data exists to support housing compliance monitoring without relying on confidential contract data — Legistar ordinances, HUD property databases, public rental listings?

7) Which Richmond institutions and organizations currently help residents navigate the planning process, and where are the gaps between their capacity and resident need?

8) What comparable tools from other cities — Councilmatic, Lookout SF, NLIHC preservation tools, or others — offer patterns Richmond teams could realistically adapt in a weekend?

9) What minimum viable prototype could meaningfully reduce resident confusion about neighborhood development within 48 hours, without pretending to be an official government system?

10) What evidence and framing would make a Thriving Neighborhoods prototype feel credible, community-centered, and continuation-worthy to judges, City staff, and neighborhood organizations?

Output
- Facts (with URLs)
- Inferences (clearly labeled)
- Unknowns
- Priority ranking of questions by research urgency
