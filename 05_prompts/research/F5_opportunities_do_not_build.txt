Opportunities F5 — What Teams Should NOT Build for Thriving Neighborhoods

Identify the project concepts that sound appealing for this pillar but are not appropriate for a hackathon, likely to cause harm, or impossible without resources that teams do not have.

Do-not-build list with reasoning:
1) Tools that predict whether a development proposal will be approved or denied
2) Tools that tell residents whether they qualify for affordable housing programs
3) Real-time displacement risk scores for neighborhoods
4) Automated compliance determination tools that generate official City findings
5) Tools that require access to confidential housing contract data or tenant-level rent rolls
6) Comprehensive citywide development platforms claiming to be official City systems
7) Tools that require real-time data pipelines, push notifications, and user accounts without a clear maintenance plan
8) Affordability calculators that claim to determine whether someone can afford a home or qualifies for workforce housing

For each, explain:
- Why teams might be tempted to build it
- What would go wrong
- What safer alternative achieves similar value

Output
- Ranked do-not-build list
- Safe alternatives for each rejected concept
- Common warning signs that a project is drifting toward do-not-build territory
