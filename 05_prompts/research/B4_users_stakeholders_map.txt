User & Stakeholder B4 — Full Stakeholder Map for Thriving Neighborhoods

Map the full ecosystem of stakeholders who interact with neighborhood development and housing compliance in Richmond.

Produce a stakeholder map covering:
- Primary users: residents affected by development proposals; City housing compliance staff
- Secondary users: Planning Commission staff and commissioners; neighborhood association leaders; housing advocates; journalists
- Enablers: City IT (Legistar system owners); GIS staff (GeoHub); HUD regional office
- Partners: PlanRVA; 1717 Collective; community development organizations; legal aid
- Decision-makers: Sharon Ebert (DCAO); City Planning Director; City Housing Director; City Council members
- Potential champions: who has both interest and institutional authority to support continued development

For each stakeholder group, identify:
- Their relationship to the core problems
- What they would need from a hackathon prototype to find it useful
- Any concerns or risks they might have about a new tool

Output
- Stakeholder map (table or narrative)
- 2–3 most important stakeholders for a hackathon team to understand
- Who to contact post-hackathon for continuation
