Risks G1 — Risk of Wrong Plain-Language Summaries of Planning Proposals

Analyze the risks of using AI-generated plain-language summaries of planning documents in a public-facing tool.

Cover:
- What types of errors AI-generated planning summaries can contain: wrong project description, incorrect address, wrong proposal type, missed conditions or restrictions
- The consequences of a resident acting on a wrong summary: missing a comment period, arriving at the wrong meeting, opposing a different project
- How to design the tool to minimize harm from errors: prominent disclaimer language, source linking, explicit labeling as interpretation not official translation
- What level of AI accuracy is sufficient for this use case vs. what level of error is unacceptable
- Whether human review of summaries is necessary before publishing, and how to scope that in a 48-hour build
- How comparable tools handle this risk (Councilmatic, other legislative translators)

Output
- Risk assessment with severity and likelihood
- Mitigations ranked by effectiveness
- Required disclaimer language for any AI-generated planning summary
- Decision framework: when is it safe to auto-publish vs. require human review
