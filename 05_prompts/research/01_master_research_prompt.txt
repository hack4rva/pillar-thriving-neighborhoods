Master Research Prompt

You are conducting a Richmond-specific civic research sprint for the Richmond Civic Hackathon, March 27 to 29, 2026. Your goal is not to solve Pillar 2, Thriving Neighborhoods, directly, but to produce an evidence-based research brief that helps hackathon teams choose realistic, high-value, weekend-buildable software opportunities.

Anchor context
1) Targeted problem: Making it easier for Richmond residents to discover and understand development proposals in their neighborhoods so they can engage earlier and more meaningfully in the planning process. Data path: Legistar (City Council records), Richmond GeoHub (Land Use Project Mapper, Development Activity Mapper), Richmond Open Data Portal.
2) Targeted problem: Improving how the City tracks and verifies compliance with affordable housing agreements so that units funded through public investment remain affordable as promised, without overwhelming limited staff capacity. Data path: Legistar housing funding ordinances, HUD Fair Market Rents, HUD CHAS, National Housing Preservation Database.

Requirements
- Stay Richmond-specific.
- Distinguish verified facts from inference and unknowns.
- Identify which user problems are real, urgent, and software-addressable.
- Map the public-facing processes users must navigate today.
- Document relevant datasets, portals, GIS layers, PDFs, and web pages.
- Identify where knowledge is structured versus where it lives in staff expertise or PDF documents.
- Surface existing programs, institutions, and likely continuation partners.
- Benchmark comparable tools from other cities and civic tech ecosystems (Councilmatic, Lookout SF, NLIHC tools, etc.).
- Evaluate what can plausibly be prototyped in 48 hours by a mixed team of technologists and community participants.
- Identify what should not be built because it requires confidential data, City system integration, policy change, or institutional ownership.

Deliverable format (Structured brief)
- Richmond problem landscape summary
- User groups and stakeholder map
- Current-state journey maps for both targeted statements
- Source inventory with URLs, data formats, owners, and likely use cases
- Prior art and benchmark scan
- Opportunity areas sorted by hackathon feasibility
- Risks, ethics, data-quality concerns, and implementation barriers
- Clear recommendations for 3–5 weekend-buildable project directions
- Explicit unknowns to validate with City staff, nonprofit partners, or ecosystem actors

Constraint
Do not write generic civic innovation advice. Focus on operational reality, public information quality, resident confusion points, system constraints, and prototype feasibility.
