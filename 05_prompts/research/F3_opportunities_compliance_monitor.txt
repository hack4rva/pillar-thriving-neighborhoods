Opportunities F3 — Affordable Housing Compliance Monitor Deep Dive

Develop a detailed opportunity analysis for the affordable housing compliance monitoring concept, given its data-readiness flag and staff-tool framing.

Cover:
- What version of this tool is actually buildable in a weekend given the data constraints
- What public data can anchor the tool (Legistar ordinances, HUD databases, FMR tables)
- How to scope the tool to be staff-facing without requiring confidential City data
- What the minimum useful demo looks like: a portfolio view of seeded developments with expiration dates and a HUD FMR comparison
- What risks must be mitigated: implying compliance determinations, using synthetic data without labeling it, overstating the tool's authority
- How to present this to judges as a staff workflow prototype, not an automated compliance system
- What the continuation pathway looks like: City housing department adoption, data hardening

Output
- Opportunity brief (500–700 words)
- What the tool can and cannot do clearly stated
- Key data decisions: which sources, how to handle the synthetic demo data
- Risks and mitigations
- Advice on whether to pursue this or stick to the development notifier
