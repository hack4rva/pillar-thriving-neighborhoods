Data Inventory D4 — HUD Fair Market Rent, CHAS, and Housing Affordability Datasets

Provide a detailed inventory of HUD's publicly available housing affordability datasets and how they apply to Richmond.

Cover:
- Fair Market Rents: what FMRs measure, how they are calculated, where to download Richmond/MSA data, API access, how frequently updated
- CHAS (Comprehensive Housing Affordability Strategy) data: what it measures, geographic granularity (city, county, census tract), how to access, data lag
- HUD Income Limits: what AMI limits apply to Richmond, where to find them, how they relate to affordable housing program eligibility
- HUD Housing Choice Voucher program data: what is publicly available for Richmond
- How to join HUD datasets to Richmond parcel or neighborhood geography

Output
- Facts (with URLs)
- Inferences (clearly labeled)
- Unknowns
- Developer quick-start: how to get Richmond FMR data in 30 minutes
- How to use FMR data in a compliance comparison tool: example calculation showing whether a committed rent level is within FMR bounds
- Limitations: what HUD data cannot tell you that a compliance tool would need
