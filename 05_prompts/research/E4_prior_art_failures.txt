Prior Art E4 — Civic Tech Failures and Abandoned Projects in Housing and Planning

Catalog civic tech projects related to housing transparency, development tracking, and neighborhood change that were built but failed, were abandoned, or caused unintended harm.

Focus on:
- Tools that were built but never adopted by residents or City staff — and why
- Projects that went stale because no one maintained the data pipeline
- Tools that gave residents incorrect information about development proposals
- Platforms that promised notifications but could not sustain the infrastructure
- Housing affordability calculators that gave misleading eligibility guidance
- Tools that were perceived as threatening by City staff or developers and were shut down

Also consider:
- What patterns of civic tech failure are most common in housing and planning contexts
- What governance and maintenance models have kept similar tools alive
- What makes Richmond's context similar to or different from cities where these failures occurred

Output
- Facts (with URLs)
- Inferences (clearly labeled)
- Unknowns
- Top 5 failure patterns most likely to affect a Thriving Neighborhoods prototype
- Mitigations for each failure pattern
