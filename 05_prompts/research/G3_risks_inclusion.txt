Risks G3 — Risk of Exclusion: Who Gets Left Out of Neighborhood Development Tools

Analyze the inclusion and equity risks of neighborhood development transparency tools in Richmond.

Cover:
- Who is most affected by development activity in Richmond (lower-income residents, renters, communities of color) and whether they are most or least likely to be reached by a web-based tool
- Digital access barriers in affected neighborhoods: smartphone access, broadband access, digital literacy
- Language access barriers: what languages are spoken in affected Richmond neighborhoods
- Whether a tool that primarily serves digitally-connected, English-speaking homeowners could reinforce existing civic engagement inequities
- How to design for inclusion: SMS-based access, multi-language content, community organization partnerships
- What offline or low-tech alternatives should be mentioned in the tool or prototype

Output
- Facts (with URLs)
- Inferences (clearly labeled)
- Unknowns
- Ranked list of inclusion risks
- Mitigations and design recommendations
- What a hackathon team should say in their demo about inclusion limitations
