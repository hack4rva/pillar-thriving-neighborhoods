Problem Landscape A5 — Rate and Nature of Neighborhood Change in Richmond

Investigate the scale and character of neighborhood change in Richmond, Virginia, as context for the Thriving Neighborhoods pillar. What is actually changing in Richmond's neighborhoods — new construction, rezoning, investment patterns — and in which neighborhoods is change most significant?

Focus on:
- Recent development activity in Richmond: new construction, major rezonings, significant Planning Commission cases
- Neighborhoods where development pressure is highest (Church Hill, Scott's Addition, Manchester, Jackson Ward, etc.)
- Housing affordability trends: rising rents, displacement pressure, homeownership barriers for workforce households
- Role of City-funded affordable housing investments in these neighborhoods
- What public data captures neighborhood change: permit data, parcel data, Legistar records, HUD CHAS

Output
- Facts (with URLs)
- Inferences (clearly labeled)
- Unknowns
- 3–5 specific Richmond neighborhoods most relevant to this pillar with brief context
- Data sources that capture neighborhood change at the local level
