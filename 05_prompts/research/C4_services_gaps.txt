Services Landscape C4 — Where Residents Are Excluded from Development Awareness

Investigate the specific service and information gaps that prevent Richmond residents from learning about and participating in development review.

Focus on:
- What services Richmond currently provides to help residents learn about development proposals (neighborhood liaisons, notification mailings, Planning Commission website, social media)
- Where these services fall short: reach limitations, language barriers, digital access gaps, timing of notifications
- Whether Richmond has a neighborhood association notification system and how reliable it is
- What other cities do to fill these gaps (email subscription services, neighborhood portals, planning staff outreach)
- Whether Richmond Planning has the staff capacity or systems to provide better proactive notification

Output
- Facts (with URLs)
- Inferences (clearly labeled)
- Unknowns
- Gap analysis: what is missing that software could address vs. what requires staffing or policy changes
- Specific services a hackathon prototype should complement rather than replace
