Risks G5 — Practical Guardrails for Thriving Neighborhoods Prototypes

Produce a practical guardrail checklist for hackathon teams building Thriving Neighborhoods prototypes. These are the non-negotiable design choices that make a prototype safe to demo and responsible to show to judges and community members.

Include guardrails for:
- Source transparency: every piece of information must show where it came from and when it was last updated
- AI summary labeling: any AI-generated content must be labeled as an interpretation and linked to the original official document
- Scope claims: tools must state explicitly what they do and do not cover
- Compliance neutrality: no tool should imply a development is compliant or non-compliant without official City determination
- Eligibility neutrality: no tool should imply a person qualifies or does not qualify for housing programs
- Data completeness: tools must state explicitly that data may not be complete or current
- Official deference: all tools must direct users to official City sources for authoritative information

Format the guardrail checklist as a pre-demo review that teams can walk through before presenting.

Output
- Guardrail checklist (pass/fail format)
- For each guardrail: the risk if violated and the fix if failing
- Required disclaimer language templates
