Missing Information Gaps — Thriving Neighborhoods

Identify the information gaps that most affect hackathon teams for this pillar. For each gap, describe what is missing, why it matters, and what would be needed to fill it.

1) Legistar API availability for Richmond. Missing: confirmation that the Granicus Web API is enabled for Richmond's Legistar instance. Matters because the development notifier and translator both depend on this. To fill: contact City IT or Planning and request the API endpoint.

2) Development case address structure in Legistar. Missing: whether Richmond's Legistar records include structured address fields or only free-text descriptions. Matters because geocoding requires a parseable address. To fill: manually inspect several Legistar records or query the API.

3) Richmond GeoHub layer freshness and access terms. Missing: confirmation that the Land Use Project Mapper and Development Activity Mapper are current and accessible via REST API. To fill: inspect the ArcGIS service metadata for last-update dates and confirm API access.

4) Affordable housing portfolio. Missing: a publicly available list of developments that have received City affordable housing grants, loans, or incentives. Matters because the compliance tool needs a known portfolio to monitor. To fill: extract from Legistar housing funding ordinances (labor-intensive) or request from City Housing department.

5) Housing compliance monitoring workflow. Missing: a description of how City housing staff currently monitors compliance — what systems they use, what data they have, where the bottlenecks are. Matters for scoping the compliance tool. To fill: interview or survey City housing staff.

6) Resident digital access and literacy in affected neighborhoods. Missing: data on smartphone ownership, broadband access, and digital literacy in Richmond neighborhoods most affected by development activity. Matters for designing accessible tools. To fill: use HUD CHAS and FCC broadband data as proxies.

7) Named departmental champion. Missing: a confirmed City contact willing to be the owner and champion for continued development of a prototype. Matters for continuation pathway. To fill: engage Sharon Ebert (DCAO) and Planning/Housing department heads.

8) Plain-language summary accuracy benchmarks. Missing: a standard for how accurate AI-generated planning summaries need to be before they are safe to publish. Matters for the translator tool. To fill: test summaries against real staff reports with a planner reviewer.

- Gaps ranked by impact on hackathon team decision-making
- Suggested mitigations for each gap
- Gaps that must be filled before building vs. gaps that can be worked around
