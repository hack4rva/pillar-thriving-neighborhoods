Data Inventory D5 — Data Quality Issues and Gaps for Thriving Neighborhoods

Assess the data quality challenges teams will face when using Legistar, Richmond GeoHub, HUD data, and other sources for Thriving Neighborhoods projects.

Cover:
- Legistar data quality: consistency of matter type classifications, reliability of address fields, completeness of records, update latency after Council actions
- GeoHub data quality: how current the development activity layers are, known gaps or errors in parcel data, projection and coordinate system notes
- HUD data quality: data lag in CHAS (2–3 year lag from census), FMR geographic granularity limitations, coverage gaps in preservation databases
- Cross-source join quality: challenges in linking Legistar records to GeoHub parcels (address format mismatches, non-geocodable addresses)
- PDF attachment quality: variability in staff report format and content that affects plain-language summarization

For each quality issue, describe:
- The severity of the impact on a hackathon prototype
- A practical mitigation strategy
- Whether the issue can be worked around or is a blocker

Output
- Facts (with URLs)
- Inferences (clearly labeled)
- Unknowns
- Ranked list of data quality risks by severity for hackathon teams
- Mitigations for each risk
