Demo Strategy I1 — Demo Archetypes for Thriving Neighborhoods Judges

Identify the demo archetypes that work best for neighborhood development and housing transparency projects in a civic hackathon judging context.

Cover:
- What judges in a civic hackathon are typically looking for: problem clarity, user empathy, data credibility, civic impact, technical execution
- Which demo structure works best for development discovery tools: the "resident who was shut out" narrative
- Which demo structure works best for housing compliance tools: the "staff workflow before and after" narrative
- How to anchor the demo in a specific Richmond neighborhood and a specific user story
- What makes a judge think "this is real" vs. "this is a toy"
- The role of data source labels, source links, and disclaimers in building judge credibility
- How to address the "what happens after the hackathon?" question

Output
- 2–3 demo archetypes with narrative structure
- Opening sentence templates for each archetype
- What to show on screen at each moment in the demo
- How to close the demo with a credible continuation ask
