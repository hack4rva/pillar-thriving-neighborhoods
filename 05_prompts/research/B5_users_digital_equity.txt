User & Stakeholder B5 — Digital Access and Equity in Affected Richmond Neighborhoods

Investigate the digital access landscape in Richmond neighborhoods most affected by development pressure and housing affordability challenges.

Focus on:
- Smartphone ownership and broadband access rates in neighborhoods like Church Hill, Jackson Ward, Gilpin Court area, Southside Richmond
- Digital literacy and comfort with web-based civic tools among lower-income and older residents
- Language access: what languages are spoken in affected neighborhoods beyond English
- Existing digital equity programs in Richmond (FCC Affordable Connectivity Program, library access, etc.)
- What device and connectivity assumptions are safe for a neighborhood development tool
- How the digital equity gap affects who can benefit from a Legistar-based tool

Use available public data sources: FCC broadband maps, Census ACS, Richmond Open Data Portal.

Output
- Facts (with URLs)
- Inferences (clearly labeled)
- Unknowns
- Design implications for a neighborhood development tool that aims to reach all affected residents
- What offline or phone-based alternatives should be mentioned in a responsible prototype
