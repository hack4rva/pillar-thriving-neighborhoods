Demo Strategy I4 — Building Demo Credibility for Thriving Neighborhoods

Identify the specific elements that make a Thriving Neighborhoods prototype feel credible and trustworthy to judges, City staff, and community members.

Cover:
- The role of real data: using real Richmond addresses, real Legistar case numbers, real neighborhood names
- The role of source transparency: displaying "from Legistar" or "from HUD" on every data element
- The role of limitation statements: acknowledging what the tool does not cover increases rather than decreases credibility
- The role of local specificity: naming specific Richmond neighborhoods and using local context
- The role of user story grounding: opening with a real type of resident (not a fictional generic user)
- The role of official links: every piece of information should have a path back to an official source
- The role of data freshness: showing when data was last updated signals responsibility
- How to avoid the common credibility killers: lorem ipsum data, generic addresses, vague impact claims

Output
- Credibility checklist for demo day
- Top 5 credibility builders for this pillar
- Top 5 credibility killers to avoid
- Required on-screen elements (source labels, disclaimers, official links)
