Opportunities F4 — Where Software Genuinely Helps in Thriving Neighborhoods

Identify the parts of the neighborhood development and housing compliance problems where software can make a real difference vs. where the core issue is organizational, political, or resource-based.

Focus on:
- Where the problem is primarily one of information access: residents cannot find what exists
- Where the problem is primarily one of information comprehension: residents cannot understand what they find
- Where the problem is primarily one of information organization: staff cannot manage what they have
- Where software can make a meaningful improvement without requiring policy change or new data
- Where software would address a symptom but not the underlying problem
- What would be more valuable than software (more staff, policy change, community organizing, etc.)

Output
- Software-addressable problems: ranked list
- Not-software-addressable problems: ranked list with brief explanation
- Where a hackathon prototype offers genuine value vs. a band-aid
- Honest framing for a judge: what this prototype does and does not solve
