Prior Art E3 — Housing Compliance Monitoring Tools

Catalog tools used by cities, housing agencies, or nonprofit organizations to monitor affordable housing compliance. Focus on tools that track whether funded developments are honoring their affordability commitments.

Include:
- HUD's own compliance monitoring tools and HEMS (HUD's monitoring system): what they cover, how cities access them
- State housing finance agency monitoring systems (VHDA in Virginia)
- Municipal housing department tools: examples of cities that have built or deployed compliance tracking software
- The NLIHC National Housing Preservation Database: how it is used for monitoring and advocacy
- Academic or nonprofit tools for tracking affordable housing at risk
- What makes housing compliance monitoring technically hard vs. organizationally hard

Output
- Facts (with URLs)
- Inferences (clearly labeled)
- Unknowns
- Patterns that Richmond's housing staff could realistically use in a staff-support tool
- What cannot be replicated without internal City data
